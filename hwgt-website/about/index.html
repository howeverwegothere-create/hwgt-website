The Story

I didn’t have a coach.
You shouldn’t have to figure it out alone either.

I started at Dell Compellent cold-calling enterprise storage deals. No playbook, no territory map — just learn the product and go find buyers. When Dell acquired Compellent in 2011, I stayed on and moved into channel sales, managing partner and reseller networks across the region and running QBRs on multi-million dollar pipeline.

After Dell I took a territory role at Cambridge Computer — rural Michigan, northern Wisconsin, pure whitespace. Built it from nothing to top 15% in revenue attainment among new reps on the team. Hit 122% of quota. The territory wasn’t good. I had to figure out how to make it work anyway, and that taught me more about selling than any quota-rich patch ever could have.

That path eventually took me through VMware and into startups where I was, essentially, the entire org chart. Blue Medora. Integration Plumbers. Companies most people have never heard of that shaped how I think about sales as much as any Fortune 500 did. I’ve been an inside rep, a field executive, a sales manager, VP of Sales, and a Chief Revenue Officer. I’ve hired and fired, hit quota and missed it, built teams from scratch and inherited messes.

What I’m doing now is what I wish someone had done for me early on: free, honest, 1:1 coaching. Not a course. Not a framework. Not a 47-email drip sequence. Real conversations with someone who’s held every seat in the revenue org and still remembers what each one felt like.

If you’re a BDR trying to break into AE, or an AE who can’t find your rhythm, reach out. No pitch. No catch. The next generation deserves better than what most companies put in the room.
