About Mike

Started making cold calls.
Ended up running revenue.

Fifteen years across every role in B2B sales — demand gen rep, BDR, AE, territory builder, BDR team director, VP of Sales, CRO. He's been in the seat he coaches from. And the seat on the other side of the table.

How it started.

Mike joined Compellent in July 2009 as a demand gen rep. Northern Ohio. Compellent was a storage company that most of the market had never heard of. The job was to find enterprise deals in accounts that weren't looking for a solution — and to get good enough at it that the company would let him close them.

He worked through partner development and BDR to outside account executive. By the time Dell acquired Compellent in 2011, he had four-plus years of reps on the board. He closed $4.5M in net-new revenue and hit quota every year he was there.

Building from nothing.

Cambridge Computers in 2014 was rural Michigan and northern Wisconsin. No existing accounts. No brand recognition outside a small regional footprint. No warm leads. He built the territory from zero. Finished at 122% of quota — top 15% in revenue attainment among all new reps.

Dell Technologies the following year, as a Solutions Sales Specialist. 121% of quota. 201% of pipeline goal. Nine months. He signed four new Dell reseller partnerships in Michigan before the year was out.

Managing the team.

Blue Medora brought Mike in as Director of Inside Global Sales in October 2015. He built an eleven-person BDR team covering North America, EMEA, and APAC. He wrote the calling playbooks. He hired the reps, trained them, and shipped them into AE roles when they were ready. Annual pipeline went from $2M to $20.8M. Blue Medora was acquired by VMware.

Running a company's revenue.

Mike joined ArcSite as VP of Sales in October 2020. ARR was under $100K. He held 70%+ annual growth for four straight years. By the time he left in mid-2024, ARR was past $10M. He built a fourteen-person team across sales, marketing, and customer success. He cracked four verticals from cold — synthetic turf, fencing, security, and pest control — writing the GTM for each one and running it cross-functionally.

Now.

Mike started However We Got Here in August 2024 as a fractional sales leadership practice. He signed four SaaS and services clients, acting as their head of sales until they could hire one. He built $4M+ in net-new pipeline across the book in twelve months.

In August 2025, he took the CRO role at Alta Vista Technology — a Sage Intacct and ERP services business — where he owns $100M in revenue across sales, marketing, partner, and customer growth. He hosts the Alta Vista Technology Podcast on YouTube alongside that role.

The coaching — office hours and 1:1 sessions with BDRs and AEs — runs alongside all of it. It's the part he's wanted to build for a long time.
